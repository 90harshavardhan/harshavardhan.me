What should be colour choices for my app and website: color Marketing and Psyche?

I have spent time wondering this question and thought maybe you too. Then I 
hit search and found there is so much to understand and so much to know about 
colors other than of course liking them. While writing this article I felt as 
if I am promoting or branding some colors — I am not. Trust me (or verify), 
if you spend some time researching colors — their uses, history and most 
importantly trends — you will be amazed to find out how we are genetically 
programmed over many years to tend to like some colors in particular. If you 
dive deeper into the reasons you might feel [voilà][1].

Do you see a connection yet? I am sure you feel something interesting — or 
maybe you already know by now. Let's delve into something more interesting.

> [A picture is worth a thousand words][6] is not a mere idiom, it is 
> actually a scientific phenomenon. This great article — [6 Powerful 
> Psychological Effects That Explain How Our Brains Tick][7] — suggests 
> that visual sense is the strongest guide of our choices in life. When we 
> like something in appearance, the rest of our logic goes into gathering 
> supporting facts and makes an innocent yet biased attempt to convince us 
> of what we are already influenced by. This [good talk][8] explains how 
> our brain constructs reality around our perception of a fact.

Having said all that, let's see what search trends suggest. Below is the 
color search trend over 15 years worldwide (since 2004). The bars represent 
Black, Red, Blue, Green and Yellow from left to right.
