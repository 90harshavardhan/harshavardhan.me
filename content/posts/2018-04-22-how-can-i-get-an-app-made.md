*Editor's note (2026): This was originally a Quora answer from April 2018. 
The four-approach framework remains valid. However, some tools mentioned 
have since evolved or shut down — Google App Maker and PhoneGap were 
discontinued, Xamarin was deprecated. Current equivalents worth exploring: 
Bubble/Glide (no-code), Flutter/React Native (frameworks), and for 
freelancers — Toptal or Upwork instead of TapChief.*

*One significant shift since 2023 — AI has fundamentally changed the 
"Least Easy" approach. Tools like GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and Claude 
Code mean you can now build a functional app with significantly less 
prior programming knowledge than before. The barrier between "Lesser Easy" 
and "Least Easy" has blurred considerably.*

This question was posted on Quora: <https://www.quora.com/How-can-I-get-an-app-made>

Below is my answer:

1. **Easiest** *(ask someone to do it for you and you watch the show)*  
   1) Contact a freelancer or firm (e.g. [Reformeta Technology Labs][1] or [TapChief][2]) who can get your app built. Tell them what you want, ask for a quotation, and once you have a plan and price, follow it up with,  
   2) Discussions on possibilities of improvement and ways of cutting down the cost, followed by,  
   3) Updated cost after incorporating the changes, and then discuss,  
   4) Terms of service for fixing issues and further enhancements in terms of features and scalability.  

   One catch — firms are generally more stable than individual freelancers in terms of availability and long-term support. If you are least interested in wandering into the forest of app-making technologies and just want to focus on your app, choose this approach.  

   *Ease of development: maximum. Cost: depends on who is making it. Enhancement and Maintenance: depends on the offering.*

2. **Lesser Easy** *(use ready-to-use lego-like tools and build it yourself)*  
   1) Define the problem clearly  
   2) Build a sketch — known as [Wireframing][3]  
   3) Register on one of the app-making online tools e.g. [Google App Maker][4], [App Maker][5]  
   4) Follow their quick guides — this involves understanding the system and testing the app  

   *Ease of development: pretty easy. Cost: almost zero unless the app uses paid services. Enhancement and Maintenance: easy.*

3. **Even Lesser Easy** *(use app-making frameworks, better material designs, more control)*  
   1) Define the problem clearly  
   2) Build a sketch — known as [Wireframing][3]  
   3) Use an app-making platform e.g. [Ionic Framework][6], [PhoneGap][7], [Xamarin][8]  
   4) Follow their quick guides — involves understanding the system and testing the app  

   *Ease of development: you need to learn a bit. Cost: depends on the platform and paid services. Enhancement and Maintenance: depends on how proficient you become.*

4. **Least Easy** *(maximum control — you essentially become the person you were talking to in approach 1)*  
   1) Define the problem clearly  
   2) Build a sketch — known as [Wireframing][3]  
   3) Decide on the app backend (e.g. REST based service using Java, Node.js or any other technology) and frontend (e.g. React, Angular)  
   4) If your app has security features, engage a security framework e.g. Spring Security  
   5) Test the app  
   6) Choose this approach only if you have one or more of the following reasons:  
      - You do it because you love to  
      - You think no one can understand your need better than you  
      - You have time, patience, and money  

   *Ease of development: pro mode, not easy unless you know programming. Cost: depends on tools and services used. Enhancement and Maintenance: if you know programming, it's all your game.*
