Today I have added the [diaspora][1] share button to my blog today. <span style="color: #99cc00;">♠</span> 🙂 <span style="color: #ff00ff;"><strong>♠</strong></span>

Though irony is, I am still waiting for the account promised by the cool n ambitious team by the end of October.[<img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-208 size-thumbnail" title="diaspora_invitation" src="/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/diaspora_invitation-150x150.jpg" alt="" width="150" height="150" />][2]Though the buzz about Diaspora can&#8217;t be termed as viral, as it could be, given the claims/possibilities/potential of it replacing Facebook or other similar platforms. Yet, how so ever far they have come, is really commendable, specially when they say that some of the coolest things in [facebook][3] and [google+][4] are inspired by [diaspora][2].

<http://blog.joindiaspora.com/2011/09/08/we-are-making-a-difference.html>

I remember when these NYU folks put their revolutionary idea at Kickstarter. I really wanted something to happen in the near future and I wanted to be a part of it.  
[<img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-thumbnail wp-image-312" src="/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/diaspora-150x150.jpg" alt="" width="150" height="150" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/diaspora-150x150.jpg 150w, /wp-content/uploads/2011/09/diaspora-100x100.jpg 100w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 150px) 100vw, 150px" />][5]  
Its amazing to see how fat they have come. I am sure, some of us would have thought them a mere bunch of high-energy university geek kids inspiring other geek kids with not much of business vision or practicality in establishing it as social networking platform model. And now I must say I can see a good momentum around the echo-system it is building. Given the fact that it is growing in a model which generally grows popularity amongst the techies of universities and other geeks, I am not sure how soon it can be a channel where-in people would start using it as much part of their lives, as much as [Facebook][3] or [twitter][6] is now. They way diaspora is growing, it reminds me of college days when Linux used to be a buzz and we used to try-out things on it, and used to love everything about it more than Windows, yet we know Windows is still far more popular platform in commons. Having said all above, I would like to conclude with a note, that I am exited about it. And post is my tribute to that exitement.
