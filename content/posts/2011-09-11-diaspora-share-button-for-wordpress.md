Today I have added the [diaspora][1] share button to my blog. 🙂

Though irony is, I am still waiting for the account promised by the cool n ambitious team by the end of October.
[<img class="aligncenter" src="/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/diaspora_invitation.jpg" />][2]

The buzz about Diaspora can't be termed as viral — as it could be, given 
the claims and potential of it replacing Facebook or other similar platforms. 
Yet, how far they have come is really commendable, specially when they say 
that some of the coolest things in [facebook][3] and [google+][4] are inspired by [diaspora][2].

<http://blog.joindiaspora.com/2011/09/08/we-are-making-a-difference.html> (dead link now)

I remember when these NYU folks put their revolutionary idea on Kickstarter. 
I really wanted something to happen in the near future and I wanted to be 
part of it.

[<img class="aligncenter" src="/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/diaspora.jpg" />][5]

Its amazing to see how fat they have come. I can see a good momentum around the echo-system it is building. Given the fact that it is growing in a model which generally grows popularity amongst the techies of universities and other geeks, I am not sure how soon it can be a channel where-in people would start using it as much part of their lives, as much as [Facebook][3] or [twitter][6] is now. They way diaspora is growing, it reminds me of college days when Linux used to be a buzz and we used to try-out things on it, and used to love everything about it more than Windows, yet we know Windows is still far more popular platform in commons. Having said all above, I would like to conclude with a note, that I am exited about it. And post is my tribute to that exitement.

You may want to try out adding the cool share-on-diaspora button. Follow the link below.
