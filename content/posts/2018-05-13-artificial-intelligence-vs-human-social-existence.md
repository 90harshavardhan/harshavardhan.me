*Rowing the boat **against** the wind — a continuous act of us humans in 
pursuit of ease, purpose, meaning, happiness, and a deeper understanding 
of self-existence. Evolution, then, is merely the byproduct.*

**"Against"** is not an immediate intent in what we do — it is an 
unavoidable phenomenon. This is evident through the history of evolution, 
where humans have always been "trying" to find or solve.

## Point to Ponder

We are stepping closer to an era where machines will coexist with humans, 
almost equally, in business, industry, and social life. In fact, they might 
be regarded as smarter — faster at doing what they are designed for. In 
the future, machines might design machines, and we will eventually lose the 
power — if not the sense of accomplishment — to decide.

Our current skill learning process is "knowledge based." We hone our skills 
to do well in the industries we work for, training ourselves on the 
knowledge accumulated since the [Industrial Revolution][13]. This closely 
resembles the world described in the book [*The World Is Flat*][14] by 
[Thomas L. Friedman][15].

## The Human Question — Who Gets Displaced, and What's Next

Over time, as machines take over what humans do and what humans are skilled 
at, humans will need to find new skills, new purpose, and a new way of 
living. The impact will likely be bigger than what we saw during the 
Industrial Revolution. I want to let my "save my life" instinct kick in 
here, and share the thoughts of [Jack Ma][16].

{{< youtube rHt-5-RyrJk >}}<br>

Jack Ma says knowledge-based study and skills won't lead a path with humans 
leading the system. A knowledge-based path means humans trying to learn 
skills based on knowledge obtained over a few hundred years — the very 
knowledge on which we are training our machines. [Knowledge-based 
approach][18] is, after all, one of the foundations on which machines 
themselves are built.

## Skill, Purpose, Education — What Stays Human?

My idea for education for the next generation resonates with Jack Ma's 
thoughts — that human values, belief in intuition, independent thinking, 
care for others, teamwork, and such notions that build a deeper connection 
with nature, will keep us unique. These are the things that will matter most 
once machines become far better than us at the skills we have spent the 
last few hundred years building.
